Haruki murakami

Haruki Murakami is a Japanese writer. His novels, essays, and short stories have been bestsellers in Japan and internationally, with his work translated into 50 languages and having sold millions of copies outside Japan. Born: January 12, 1949 (age 74 years), Fushimi Ward, Kyoto, Japan Influenced by: Kenzaburō Ōe, Natsume Sōseki, Ludwig Wittgenstein, Sigmund Freud, Carl Jung, Immanuel Kant Spouse: Yoko Murakami (m. 1971) Nominations: Neustadt International Prize for Literature, MORE Albums: Hard-Boiled Wonderland und das Ende der Welt (Ungekürzt)

Yukio Mishima

Kimitake Hiraoka, also known as Yukio Mishima, was a Japanese author, poet, playwright, actor, model, Shintoist, nationalist, and founder of the Tatenokai, an unarmed civilian militia. Mishima is considered one of the most important writers of the 20th century. Born: January 14, 1925, Yotsuya-ku Died: November 25, 1970, Japan Ground Self-Defense Force Influenced by: Yasunari Kawabata, Osamu Dazai, MORE Spouse: Yoko Hiraoka (m. 1958–1970) Children: Iichiro Hiraoka, Noriko Tomita

Yoko tawada

Yōko Tawada is a Japanese writer currently living in Berlin, Germany. She writes in both Japanese and German. Born: March 23, 1960 (age 63 years), Nakano City, Tokyo, Japan Awards: National Book Award for Translated Literature Education: Waseda University, University of Zurich, University of Hamburg Movies: Under Snow Nominations: National Book Award for Translated Literature Nationality: Japanese

Mieko Kawakami

Mieko Kawakami is the author of the internationally best-selling novel, Breasts and Eggs, a New York Times Notable Book of the Year and one of TIME's Best 10 Books of 2020. Born: August 29, 1976 (age 46 years), Osaka, Osaka, Japan Spouse: Kazushige Abe (m. 2011) Albums: 頭の中と世界の結婚, 夢みる機械 Influenced by: Haruki Murakami, Ichiyō Higuchi, Hitoshi Nagai Parents: Rie Kawakami Siblings: Toshiaki Kawakami, Sachiko Kawakami
